# Digital Literacy Project

This repository serves as a comprehensive portfolio for the project I did on **Digital Literacy** . It documents a journey through the fundamental pillars of modern digital citizenship: from technical proficiency and professional branding to online safety and ethical communication. A deep-dive into this project helps you navigate the digital world — from creating professional online profiles, to communicating safely and responsibly online.

## 📌 Project Overview

The objective of this project is to demonstrate a holistic understanding of how to navigate the digital world safely and professionally. It covers five distinct "Tasks" that range from technical coding exercises to social engineering case studies.

## 📂 Sub-Projects & Task Details

### [Task 1] Building a Safer Online Space

  * **Format:** Presentation / Educational Guide
  * **Focus:** Core Digital Literacy components.
  * **Highlights:** \* Definition of Digital Literacy: Understanding technology, evaluating info, and digital creation.
      * Practical tips for "Thinking Before You Share."
      * Essential security practices: Strong passwords, recognizing phishing, and preventing cyberbullying.

### [Task 2] Professional Digital Identity

  * **Format:** Portfolio Showcase
  * **Focus:** Establishing a professional presence across major tech platforms.
  * **Platforms Included:**
      * **GitHub:** Showcasing a foundation in Python and a commitment to mastering Data Structures and Algorithms.
      * **LinkedIn:** Professional networking as a GenAI and ML enthusiast at VIT Bhopal University.
      * **Kaggle:** Documenting the start of a data science journey.

### [Task 3] Technical Platforms & Awareness

  * **Format:** Technical Documentation & Assessment
  * **Focus:** Practical application of skills and knowledge verification.
  * **Components:**
      * **Coding Practice:** Successful implementation of the "Solve Me First" algorithm in Python 3 on HackerRank.
      * **Digital Literacy Quiz:** A self-designed assessment covering Two-Factor Authentication (2FA), digital footprints, and identifying social engineering. The google form of
                                   the assessment is hence attached - https://forms.gle/xSAKPamXvndpTYkn6

### [Task 4] Professional Communication 
Part - A : Email etiquette
  * **Format:** Correspondence Samples
  * **Focus:** Mastering the art of formal digital communication.
  * **Scenarios:**
      * **Academic:** A respectful request for an assignment extension due to medical reasons (Jaundice).
      * **Career:** A formal internship inquiry addressed to the NLP team at Cospin, highlighting skills in AI, ML, and Prolog.
Part - B : Social media checklist
  * **Format:** Markdown checklist
  * **Focus:** Learning the do's and don'ts of using social media as a college student

### [Task 5] Cybercrime Case Study
Part - A : A case study on UPI & QR scams
  * **Format:** Analytical Report
  * **Focus:** Social Engineering in the Indian financial landscape.
  * **Key Findings:**
      * Analysis of the "Request Money" vs. "Send Money" deception.
      * The psychology of the "Lure" (using trust-building pretexts like posing as an army officer).
      * Impact analysis: Financial loss and systemic consequences in the Indian digital economy.

Part - B : Stay safe online Checklist
  * **Format:** Markdown Checklist
  * **Focus:** Maintaining a positive and safe digital footprint.
  * **Key Guidelines:**
      * **The Billboard Test:** "Would I be comfortable seeing this on a giant billboard in the center of campus?"
      * **Privacy Audits:** Regularly checking old content and tags.
      * **Cyber-venting:** Avoiding unprofessional public criticism of institutions or individuals.
