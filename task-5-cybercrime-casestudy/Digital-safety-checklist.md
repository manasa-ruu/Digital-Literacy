🛡️ **The Digital Safety Checklist**

 1) **Secure Your UPI Transactions** Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. Scammers often send "Collect Requests" masquerading as refunds or prize winnings.
     Remember that the PIN is only for sending money, never for receiving.
 2) **Verify Before You Scan** When using QR codes at local vendors or stalls, verify the name displayed on your app matches the merchant. Fraudsters sometimes
      paste their own QR stickers over the original ones.
 3) **Lock Your Biometrics (Aadhaar)** Use the mAadhaar app or the UIDAI portal to lock your biometric information. This prevents unauthorized use of your fingerprint
      or iris data for SIM swapping or fraudulent KYC authentication.
 4) **Beware of "Part-Time Job" Scams** Ignore WhatsApp or Telegram messages offering high pay for simple tasks like "liking YouTube videos." These are often precursors
      to "task-based" financial scams.
 5) **Use Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** Enable 2FA on your university email, Instagram, and WhatsApp. Use an authenticator app rather than just SMS to protect
      yourself against SIM-cloning attacks.
 6) **Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Banking** Campus or café Wi-Fi is great for research, but never log into your bank portal or shopping sites on open networks. If you
      must, use a reputable VPN to encrypt your connection.
 7) **Practice "Social Media Hygiene"** Set your profiles to private and be wary of "Follow Requests" from accounts that look like copies of your friends' profiles
      (impersonation accounts used for SOS money scams).
 8) **Check the "S" in HTTPS** Before entering any credentials on a college portal or a government site, ensure the URL starts with https://. Look for the padlock
      icon in the address bar to ensure the connection is encrypted.

🚨 **How to Report Cybercrime**

If you or someone you know falls victim to a digital scam or online harassment, do not hesitate to use the official government channels:
Immediate Financial Fraud: Call the National Helpline 1930 instantly to freeze the transaction.
General Reporting: Log your complaint at the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal: cybercrime.gov.in
