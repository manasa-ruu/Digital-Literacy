Social Media Do's and Don'ts checklist :

Do's : 
1) Audit your privacy settings regularly. Ensure you know exactly who can see your posts, especially older content from high school.
2) Think before you "Like" or Share. Remember that interactions with controversial or hateful content are often visible to others.
3) Use your platform for networking. Follow industry leaders, join professional groups, and share your project milestones or creative wins.
4) Practice "The Billboard Test." Before posting, ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable seeing this on a giant billboard in the center of campus?"
5) Give credit where it’s due. If you are sharing someone else's art, research, or photography, always tag the original creator.

Dont's :
1) Don't post illegal or policy-violating content. Avoid photos or text depicting underage drinking, drug use, or academic dishonesty.
2) Don't engage in "cyber-venting." Avoid trashing professors, internships, or your university, as these can be flagged by future recruiters.
3) Don't share sensitive personal info. Keep your physical location, student ID number, and private schedule off public feeds to ensure your safety.
4) Don't get into "comment section" wars. Heated arguments rarely end well and can leave a permanent digital trail of unprofessionalism.
5) Don't ignore your "tagged" photos. Untag yourself from photos that don't align with the image you want to project professionally.
